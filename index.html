Letter to Mercy

Dear Mercy,
I have been thinking about doing this for a little while, not because the words are hard to find, but because what you mean to me cannot be reduced to something small.

On the 2nd of September 2024, when I resumed as an intern in the company you worked in the ancient city of Benin. From my very first days you were one colleague who showed genuine care and concern. Before I knew it, you had moved from the office into my heart.

I quietly observed the sacrifices you made, going out of your way in getting things done. Your aura carries such peace. You cannot stand disorderliness, and you will burn yourself out without hesitation if needed. You are a burning and shining light indeed, mon ami.

This is not an anniversary, not a birthday, not New Year, nor is it an obvious special day, but must there be a special reason for me to express my gratitude? Your selflessness is rare, and it deserves to be appreciated every single day!

Your words and your presence have done something deep inside me over time. They have built a quiet confidence in me, a stronger resilience, and a comforting sense that I am not entirely alone in this journey. Across the kilometres and the different time zones, even while carrying your own weight and fighting your own battles, you still show up. You call. You check in. You speak with that perfect mix of honesty and warmth that only you have. You're effortlessly humourous, even in heavy moments, that is a gift I don’t think you fully realise.

I see all of it: your wisdom, your wit, your loyalty that never needs to announce itself, and the way you keep showing up even when it costs you something. None of it has gone unnoticed. None of it has been taken for granted.

I want you to know that you are rare. The world is better because you are in it. And I am specifically, concretely, measurably better because you are in my life.

God bless you, keep you, uphold you, and strengthen you. Keep pushing, my guy! We will see each other at the top!

With every ounce of sincerity,
Praise-Emmanuel.
